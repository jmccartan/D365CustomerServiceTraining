
# The 10 Most Important Concepts in Dynamics 365 Customer Service

## 1. Case

The **core record** in Customer Service.

A **Case** represents a customer issue or request.

Examples:

- Support ticket
    
- Product issue
    
- Billing question
    
- Service request
    

Key features:

- Status (Active, Resolved)
    
- Priority
    
- Owner
    
- Timeline of interactions
    

Everything in the system is ultimately **about managing cases**.

---

## 2. Queue

A **Queue** is where work waits to be handled.

Think of it like a **support inbox**.

Examples:

- Tier 1 Support Queue
    
- Billing Support Queue
    
- VIP Customer Queue
    

Queues allow:

- Routing work to teams
    
- Monitoring backlog
    
- Work distribution
    

---

## 3. Routing Rules

Routing determines **how cases get assigned**.

Common routing logic:

- Product category
    
- Customer tier
    
- Language
    
- Geography
    

Example:

If case type = billing  
→ route to Billing Queue

Modern implementations often use **AI-based routing**.

---

## 4. Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

SLAs enforce **response and resolution commitments**.

Example SLA:

|Metric|Target|
|---|---|
|First response|2 hours|
|Case resolution|24 hours|

If targets are missed:

- Alerts trigger
    
- Escalations occur
    

---

## 5. Knowledge Base

A **knowledge base** stores solutions for common problems.

Examples:

- Troubleshooting guides
    
- FAQs
    
- Product instructions
    

Benefits:

- Agents can resolve cases faster
    
- Customers can self-serve
    

Knowledge articles are often **linked directly to cases**.

---

## 6. Entitlements

Entitlements define **what level of support a customer is allowed**.

Examples:

- Standard support
    
- Premium support
    
- Warranty coverage
    

Example entitlement:

Customer receives 10 support incidents per year

---

## 7. Customer Service Workspace

This is the **modern agent interface**.

It provides:

- Case timeline
    
- Knowledge suggestions
    
- Copilot assistance
    
- Multiple conversations
    

It replaces the old fragmented agent experience.

---

## 8. Omnichannel

Omnichannel lets support happen across **multiple communication channels**.

Examples:

- Chat
    
- Email
    
- SMS
    
- WhatsApp
    
- Voice
    
- Social media
    

All conversations are unified inside the same agent workspace.

---

## 9. Automation

Automation reduces manual work.

Common automations:

- Auto-create cases from emails
    
- Escalate overdue cases
    
- Auto-assign cases
    
- Trigger workflows
    

Automation typically uses:

- **Power Automate**
    
- **Business rules**
    
- **AI routing**
    

---

## 10. Copilot / AI

The newest layer of the platform.

AI capabilities include:

- Case summaries
    
- Suggested replies
    
- Knowledge article recommendations
    
- Automated conversation bots
    

Copilot dramatically improves **agent productivity**.
